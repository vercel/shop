## Composable commerce

Composable commerce, headless commerce: both of these phrases describe roughly the same architectural decision, which is to let your commerce platform handle the commerce (inventory, checkout, payments, and the tax math you never want to be creative about) while you build the storefront yourself. Your framework, your infrastructure, your opinions about how a URL should work. People arrive here from different directions: faster pages, real control over product and cart experiences, or a site that can evolve without waiting on a new platform release. And increasingly, the demand for agentic commerce features - AI-powered search, conversational product discovery, and dynamic recommendations - all of which live in your frontend and all of which are evolving fast enough that you need the flexibility to adopt them as they arrive.

## The complexity gap

Complexity has always been part of this story. When the platform isn't rendering your pages, you're responsible for everything it used to do for you: routing, caching, image optimization, and the dozen things you didn't realize were handled until they weren't. Templates and starters have tried to close this gap, but they can be undernurtured, or they're so opinionated that customizing them costs you most of the time you saved by not starting from scratch.

## Agentic development

The way teams write code has changed. Agentic development tools keep getting meaningfully better, and adoption grows by the day: agents that can scaffold across multiple files, hold the full context of a framework, an API, and a deployment target at once, and generate working software rather than suggestions. Teams using these tools are shipping features faster and with more confidence than ever.

## Why Vercel Shop

This is why we built Vercel Shop: an agent-first starter for building Shopify storefronts with Next.js, designed for how builders actually build now. Drop in your Shopify credentials and go. The project ships with robust skills that teach your agent how the codebase works, how the Shopify Storefront API behaves, and how to make changes that deploy cleanly to [Vercel](https://vercel.com).

## Make it yours

What you do with it from there is the point. Swap in your CMS. Connect your search provider. Integrate whatever you're convinced matters. We've been opinionated about the pieces that should be stable and open about the pieces that should move. The interesting parts are waiting for you.
